## Version 0.6.2 is here

We’ve been working hard to make Cryyer more flexible and reliable. This latest release focuses on giving you more control over your data and making the automated parts of your workflow even smoother.

### What’s new

- **GitHub Gist Subscriber Store**: You can now store and manage your subscriber list using a GitHub Gist. This makes it much easier to update your audience without needing to manage complex databases.
- **Flexible Pipelines**: We’ve added a new way to choose your workflow. Whether you want to send weekly digests, release-specific updates, or both, you can now toggle these easily.
- **Smarter AI Drafts**: We’ve improved how Cryyer talks to the AI to ensure your email drafts are generated reliably, even if the first attempt hits a snag.
- **Faster Setup**: The initialization process is now much quicker and can run without constant manual input, making it easier to add Cryyer to new projects.
- **A New Name on NPM**: We’ve moved! You can now find the project under `@atriumn/cryyer`.

### Why it matters

These updates mean less time fighting with configuration and more time sharing your progress. By moving subscriber management to Gists and refining the automated drafting process, Cryyer handles the busy work so you can stay focused on building.

You can find the full release notes on [GitHub](https://github.com/atriumn/cryyer/releases).
